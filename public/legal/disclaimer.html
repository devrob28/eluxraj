2. Alternative Asset-Specific Risks

2.1 Illiquidity Risk

Alternative assets (precious metals, collectibles, art, private equity, real estate) are generally less liquid than publicly traded securities. You may not be able to sell these assets quickly or at desired prices. Market conditions, lack of buyers, and transaction costs can significantly impact your ability to exit positions.

2.2 Valuation Uncertainty

Alternative assets often lack transparent, real-time pricing. Valuations may be subjective, infrequent, or based on limited market data. Appraisals can vary significantly between experts. The true market value may only be known when an asset is actually sold.

2.3 Market Volatility

Alternative asset markets can experience extreme volatility. Prices may fluctuate dramatically due to supply/demand imbalances, economic conditions, geopolitical events, regulatory changes, or shifts in investor sentiment.

2.4 Concentration Risk

Alternative assets may represent significant concentrations of wealth in single items or asset classes. Lack of diversification increases the impact of adverse events affecting specific holdings.

2.5 Fraud & Authenticity Risk

The alternative asset market has risk of fraud, counterfeiting, and misrepresentation. Authentication, provenance verification, and condition assessment require expert knowledge. We cannot guarantee the authenticity or quality of any assets.

2.6 Storage & Insurance Costs

Physical assets require secure storage, insurance, and maintenance. These costs can be substantial and reduce overall returns. Loss, theft, or damage may not be fully covered by insurance.

2.7 Regulatory & Tax Risk

Alternative assets may be subject to complex regulatory requirements and tax treatment. Rules vary by jurisdiction and asset type. Changes in laws or regulations can adversely affect asset values and liquidity.

2.8 Transaction Costs

Buying and selling alternative assets typically involves significant transaction costs including dealer spreads, auction fees, appraisal costs, shipping, and insurance. These costs can substantially reduce returns.

4. Specific Asset Class Risks

4.1 Precious Metals

  • Prices highly sensitive to currency fluctuations, interest rates, and inflation expectations
  • No dividends or interest income
  • Storage and insurance costs
  • Potential for confiscation or restrictions in certain jurisdictions

4.2 Rare Coins & Collectibles

  • Extremely illiquid markets with high bid-ask spreads
  • Authenticity and grading disputes
  • Subject to changing tastes and collector preferences
  • Condition deterioration over time
  • High transaction costs (20-40% spreads common)

4.3 Fine Art

  • Highly subjective valuations
  • Very illiquid with unpredictable sale timelines
  • Authentication challenges and forgery risk
  • Significant auction fees (up to 25%)
  • Climate control and conservation costs

4.4 Private Equity

  • Long lock-up periods (5-10 years typical)
  • High minimum investments
  • Limited transparency
  • Management fees and carried interest reduce returns
  • Capital calls may be required

4.5 Real Estate

  • Illiquidity and high transaction costs
  • Property maintenance and management responsibilities
  • Local market conditions and regulatory changes
  • Tenant risk and vacancy periods
  • Leverage amplifies both gains and losses
